# Thesis Direction Rubric

A good thesis direction or proposal seed is:

- grounded in at least one chair/researcher signal or recent paper
- narrow enough for a bachelor or master thesis conversation
- explicit about method and prerequisite assumptions
- phrased as a hypothesis to validate with a supervisor
- useful even if the final topic is co-created later
- connected to the student's research taste, not only to a chair keyword

Avoid:

- broad field labels with no research question
- guaranteed topic availability
- claims about private datasets or hardware access unless sourced
- generic titles that could be sent to any chair
