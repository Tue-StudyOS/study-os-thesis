# Thesis Contact Email Rubric

A strong first-contact email:

- is short enough to read quickly
- names a specific chair/person and one relevant research signal
- connects the student's background to that signal
- asks for a conversation or guidance, not an immediate commitment
- acknowledges that the concrete thesis question can be co-created

Avoid:

- generic "I am interested in AI" phrasing
- long autobiographies
- unsupported claims about grades or skills
- pressure to supervise
- attaching large files unless requested
